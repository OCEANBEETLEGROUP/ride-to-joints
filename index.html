01

Kamakura → Hamamatsu

Thursday, April 10 — Rainy Day Van Cruise, Conveyor Sushi & Izakaya Night

☔ Weather Update

Day 1 is forecast for heavy rain, so we're loading HELL FIRE and SHOGUN into the van and cruising straight to Hamamatsu together. Think of it as the storytelling intro — the shop, the sushi, the izakaya night. The real ride starts Day 2, when the choppers hit the road from Hamamatsu to Nagoya.

10:00
OCEANBEETLE Kamakura
Walk into the shop where these machines were born. Meet RSK — the man who built them. Touch the metal, hear the story, pick your chopper. Then we load HELL FIRE and SHOGUN into the van together.
10:30
Store Shoot
Thirty minutes inside the shop to capture the atmosphere — the bikes, the build, the people. The quiet moment before the road.
11:30
Departure — Van Cruise West
Six of us, two bikes in the van. Onto the highway: Chigasaki Kaigan → Shin-Shonan Bypass → Ken-O Expressway → Tomei → Numazu. Rain on the windows, choppers in the back. Pure Japanese road trip mood.
13:00
Kura Sushi Mishima — Conveyor Belt Lunch
Sushi glides past on the belt. Touch-screen ordering. The freshest fish you've ever tasted. Clear five empty plates into the slot and Bikkura-Pon drops a surprise capsule toy. The most fun lunch in Japan.
14:00
Shin-Tomei → Hamamatsu
130 km cruise west on Japan's newest expressway. Mountain passes through the rain, warm cups of vending-machine coffee at the rest stops. Arrive in Hamamatsu by mid-afternoon.
16:00
Check-In — Route Inn Hamamatsu
Bikes stay in the van. Crew heads inside. Rest, shower, change, breathe. You've got two hours before the night begins.
18:30
Izakaya Night — Koronagi
Counter seats. Charcoal-grilled everything. Simmering oden pots. Cold beer, sake, loud laughter. Then hit the streets — the night is still young in Hamamatsu.
02

Hamamatsu → Nagoya

Friday, April 11 — Lake Views, Ramen Rituals, Kimono & Castle

09:15
Kominka Coffee
Morning coffee in a restored 100-year-old farmhouse. Sunlight through paper screens. A quiet start before the final push.
10:15
Lake Hamana — Shibuki Bridge
A crimson bridge arcs over the lake like something from a Miyazaki film. Line up the bikes against the red lacquer and let the scene speak for itself.
10:35
Highway Cruise → Nagoya
90 minutes on the Tomei Expressway, 100 km west. Rest stop at NEOPASA Okazaki if you need it. Roll into Nagoya by noon.
12:00
Ichiran Ramen
Solo booths. A custom order sheet. A bamboo curtain lifts — the most perfect bowl of tonkotsu ramen appears. This isn't lunch. It's a ceremony.
13:30
Kimono Experience
Trade your riding jacket for a hand-tied kimono. Then step outside — because there's nothing more iconic than a kimono-clad rider standing next to a chopper.
14:45
Nagoya Castle
One of Japan's most famous castles, crowned with golden dolphins. Explore in kimono — where samurai history meets chopper culture.

Day 03 — JOINTS 2026

Saturday, April 12 — Japan's Largest Custom Motorcycle Show

09:30 – 16:00
Port Messe Nagoya
The final destination. JOINTS is where Japan's finest custom builders reveal their latest work — choppers, bobbers, hot rods, and machines that defy category. Walk the floor, meet the legends. You didn't just attend. You arrived.
